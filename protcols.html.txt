<section class="protocol-container">

    <div class="protocol-card">
        <h2>Principle</h2>
        <p>
            Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) is a technique used to amplify
            a specific region of DNA using sequence-specific primers and
            a thermostable DNA polymerase.
        </p>
    </div>

    <div class="protocol-card">
        <h2>Materials Required</h2>
        <ul>
            <li>Template DNA</li>
            <li>Forward and reverse primers</li>
            <li>dNTP mixture</li>
            <li>Reaction buffer</li>
            <li>DNA polymerase (Taq / Phusion)</li>
            <li>Nuclease-free water</li>
        </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="protocol-card">
        <h2>Procedure</h2>
        <ol>
            <li>Prepare PCR reaction mixture on ice.</li>
            <li>Mix gently and briefly centrifuge.</li>
            <li>Run PCR using appropriate thermal cycling conditions.</li>
            <li>Analyze amplified product using agarose gel electrophoresis.</li>
        </ol>
    </div>

    <div class="protocol-card">
        <h2>Expected Result</h2>
        <p>
            A DNA band of expected size should be observed on agarose gel.
        </p>
    </div>

    <div class="protocol-card">
        <h2>Troubleshooting</h2>

        <table class="table">
            <tr>
                <th>Problem</th>
                <th>Possible Reason</th>
                <th>Solution</th>
            </tr>
            <tr>
                <td>No band</td>
                <td>Low template or incorrect annealing temperature</td>
                <td>Increase template concentration or optimize annealing temperature</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
                <td>Multiple bands</td>
                <td>Non-specific primer binding</td>
                <td>Increase annealing temperature or redesign primers</td>
            </tr>
        </table>
    </div>
